Work in Progress

A Computational Method to Generate Family of Extreme Value Volatility Estimators

An algorithm to construct a family of unbiased extreme value volatility estimators is developed when the log prices of assets follow simple Brownian motion with drift and jump. From this family of estimators, the minimum variance estimator can be computed and is found to be competitive to the estimators of Parkinson (1980) and Yang Zhang (2000).

Forecasting Inflation in India using Divisia and Simple Sum Monetary Aggregates

This paper empirically tests whether the inclusion of monetary aggregates in inflation forecasting models helps the forecasting ability for India from 1994-95:Q1 to 2016-17:Q4.

Projects

Heterogeneous Effects of Monetary Policy Shocks on Sectoral Output in India

The study attempts to analyze effects of monetary policy shocks on sectoral output. By means of a Vector-Autoregressive model, sector-specific responses to monetary policy shocks are observed based on quarterly data for the period 1996-97:Q1 to 2014-15:Q2.

Predictable Behaviour of Security Returns

Used panel data model to reject the hypothesis that stock prices follow random walk. CMIE prowess data on security returns of 186 Bombay Stock Exchange listed group A companies was used.

Time Varying Parameter Model for Modelling Changing Conditional Variance

Used a time varying parameter model to model monetary growth function and Kalman Filtering was applied to estimate the model. This was a replication of a paper written by Kim and Nelson.
